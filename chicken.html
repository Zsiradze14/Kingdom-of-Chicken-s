Etymology

According to Merriam-Webster, the term "rooster" (i.e. a roosting bird) originated in the mid- or late 18th century as a euphemism to avoid the sexual connotation of the original English "cock",
[22][23][24] and is widely used throughout North America. "Roosting" is the action of perching aloft to sleep at night, which is done by both sexes.
